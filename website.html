Brownies were created in the late 19th century by accident when a baker forgot to add baking powder, resulting in a dense, fudgy treat that quickly became a beloved dessert!Brownies became popular in the early 20th century when a Chicago baker added melted chocolate to a cookie recipe, creating a rich, chewy dessert that's now enjoyed worldwide in many variations!In 1893, the first published brownie recipe appeared in the "Boston Cooking School Cook Book," with a unique ingredient: unsweetened chocolate, which gives brownies their signature rich, decadent flavor.

Pizza originated in Italy, but the first pizzeria in the U.S. opened in 1905 in New York. Today, more than 3 billion pizzas are sold worldwide every year!The world’s largest pizza, measuring 1261.65 square meters, was made in Rome in 2012. It was called “Ottavia” and was gluten-free to promote health-conscious eating alongside its massive size!Pizza was originally a street food in Naples, Italy, served to the poor. It became globally popular after World War II, especially after American soldiers tasted it while stationed in Italy!
